## Purpose

This blueprint is for BAAD photography-led and visual-culture articles where photographs, image sequences, archives, photographic practice, or the act of looking are central.

The blueprint should prevent photography articles from becoming generic exhibition listings, architecture articles, or lightly rewritten source summaries. It should make the photograph, series, archive, viewpoint, or photographer’s act of looking the article’s entry point.

## Five photography engines

The blueprint now recognizes five main opening engines:

1. **Photographer’s search**  
   What the photographer keeps looking for, returning to, or trying to notice.

2. **Repeated subject**  
   A visual subject, object, gesture, surface, window, house, street, face, or detail that appears again and again.

3. **Place / architecture / atmosphere**  
   A building, landscape, city, interior, or environment seen through the camera.

4. **Historical or social role**  
   What photography preserves, documents, reveals, remembers, or makes visible.

5. **Everyday observation / chance encounter**  
   Strange, funny, awkward, surprising, or easily missed scenes from public life.
